    Overview

    The “search” box at the top-right of every page in CourSys is usually ignored because users have come to assume it's useless. It isn't.

    Things Searched

    The search will automatically search for any of these things that seem relevant:

    • Courses you were in: students, TAs, and instructors can search for things like “cmpt 120” or “programming” to find course offerings.
    • Course pages: anybody can search for content on course pages that are visible to them, e.g. “software help”.
    • Discussion: anybody can search for course discussion content, e.g. “uploading problem”.
    • Students in courses: instructors and TAs can search for students in their courses. This is likely the most useful search feature…

    Student Search

    The search for students in your courses is very powerful when trying to find a relevant student. For example:

    • The student who just emailed me from example@sfu.ca: search “example” or “example@sfu.ca”.
    • The student with a Gmail address but whose name is John Doe (or maybe Jonathan?): search “John Doe”.
    • The student who goes by “Tony Chan” but has an official Chinese name I don't remember: search “Tony Chan” (if SIMS knows the preferred name).
    • Um… his name is Frank and he is in CMPT 120: search “Frank 120”.
    • Er… that girl named Sarah (or maybe it was Sara) who was in my class about two years ago: search “Sarah {{ two_years }}”.
    • Dammit… his name is Curtis… isn't that enough? Yes: search “Curtis”.
