    As your Faculty’s Departmental Administrator for {{ CourSys }}, you recognize that {{ CourSys }} access will provide you and those you assign a role to with the ability to view and retrieve personal and confidential data.  As such, as an assigned Departmental Administrator in {{ CourSys }}, you are agreeing to the following when assigning individuals in your Faculty to a particular role:

    • No role account will be assigned to an SFU employee who does not already have SIMS access, and who has not completed the SIMS confidentially agreement
    • No role account will be assigned to an SFU employee who does not require the data to perform their work at the University.
    • Data provided via {{ CourSys }} shall not be re-purposed in any way.
    • Roles are to only be assigned to those SFU employees for the length of their contract.  Please note, all roles expire after a maximum of one (1) year. 
    • You are agreeing that you have an exit checklist for each employee in your Faculty, and upon their leaving their position, removal of their {{ CourSys }} access will be completed in a timely manner
    • You recognize that {{ CourSys }} access will provide you and those you assign a role to with the ability to view and retrieve personal data and that you have a legal obligation to protect personal information to which you have access through {{ CourSys }} - and by extension, a subset of the SIMS Reporting Database.
    • In accordance with the Freedom of Information and Protection of Privacy Act , and University Policy I 10.04, you agree to protect all personal information to which you have access to, and those you provide {{ CourSys }} access to during the term your appointment with the University.
    • You agree to use information contained in {{ CourSys }} only for the purposes for which the personal information was collected and for purposes consistent with your job duties.
    • You agree that you will disclose personal information only as permitted by law.

    Please note

    The core {{ CourSys }} data is a copy of data contained in the SIMS reporting database, which in its basic form consists of hundreds of tables and complex interrelationships. Frequently, it is not obvious how the data is structured nor is the meaning of individual data elements. All users, including those who only run queries produced by others, should be trained to verify data as results may change as new parameters (user groups, academic plans, etc.) are added/changed/deleted in the database. The impact of such changes on query results may not be obvious and hence the use of such information may result in erroneous or inappropriate actions. The Department recognizes and accepts any and all consequences of the Applicant's use of this data.
